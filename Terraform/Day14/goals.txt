DAY 14 — GOVERNANCE & POLICY ENFORCEMENT
==========================================

PRIMARY GOALS
-------------
1. Create Azure Policy Definitions via Terraform — custom rules for your subscription
2. Create a Policy Initiative (Set) — group related policies into one assignment
3. Assign policies at Resource Group scope via Terraform
4. Test enforcement — attempt non-compliant deployment and confirm it is blocked
5. Understand the difference between Audit, Deny, and DeployIfNotExists effects

LEARNING GOALS
--------------
- Understand what Azure Policy is at the API level
- Know why policy scope matters: subscription vs resource group vs management group
- Understand policy effects: Deny blocks, Audit logs, DeployIfNotExists remediates
- Know the difference between built-in and custom policy definitions
- Be able to explain policy initiatives and why they exist

POLICIES YOU WILL ENFORCE TODAY
---------------------------------
1. Require mandatory tags: environment, owner, costCenter — Effect: Deny
2. Restrict allowed locations: centralindia, westeurope only — Effect: Deny
3. Deny public storage accounts — Effect: Deny
4. Audit resources without diagnostic settings — Effect: Audit

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE TODAY
-------------------------------
- terraform apply succeeds — all policy definitions, initiative, and assignments created
- Attempting to create a storage account in East US fails at plan/apply time
- Attempting to create a resource without required tags fails
- Attempting to create a public storage account fails
- You can explain every policy block you wrote — no copy/paste blindness
